Family Deductible vs.
Individual Deductible.

This is the number that trips up most families comparing family health insurance plans - here's how it actually works, and how many members it covers.

I

Individual
Deductible

Each covered person has their own deductible amount that applies to their own claims before the plan starts paying its share for that person.

F

Family
Deductible

A single household cap - once combined family out-of-pocket spending reaches it, the plan pays its share for every member, even if one person hasn't hit their individual amount.

#

Members
Covered

Most family health insurance plans cover a spouse or partner and dependent children up to age 26 - your advisor confirms who's eligible on your plan before you enroll.

Family Health Insurance
Questions, Answered.

What's the difference between a family deductible and an individual deductible?

An individual deductible applies separately to each covered person. A family deductible is a combined household cap - once your family's total out-of-pocket spending reaches it, the plan starts paying its share for every member, even those who haven't individually hit their own amount.

How many members can be covered on a family health insurance plan?

Most family health insurance plans cover a policyholder, a spouse or domestic partner, and dependent children - typically up to age 26. Exact eligibility rules vary by carrier and plan, so a licensed advisor can confirm who qualifies on any plan you're considering.

Does family health insurance include pediatric dental and vision?

Pediatric dental and vision are essential health benefits under the ACA, so most qualified family health insurance plans build them in for covered children. Coverage details still vary by plan, so confirm exactly what's included before you enroll.

Can I add a newborn or new spouse outside of Open Enrollment?

Yes. A birth, adoption, or marriage triggers a Special Enrollment Period, giving you roughly 60 days to add the new dependent to your family health insurance plan without waiting for the next Open Enrollment window.

Is one family plan cheaper than separate individual plans?

For most households, bundling everyone under one family health insurance plan costs less than buying a separate individual policy for each person, and it consolidates you toward a single family deductible. A licensed advisor can run the comparison against your actual household.

Is the Leo Source advisor service free?

Yes. Our concierge advisors are compensated directly by carriers - there's no separate fee, retainer, or commission added to your premium, subsidized or not.
