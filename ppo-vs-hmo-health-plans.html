PPO And HMO:
What's Actually Different.

Both PPO plans and HMO plans can be solid ACA-compliant coverage - the difference is how you access care, not whether you're covered.

1

Network
Access

PPO plans cover in-network and out-of-network care (out-of-network usually costs more). HMO plans generally only cover in-network care except in emergencies.

2

Referrals To
See A Specialist

PPO plans let you go straight to a specialist. HMO plans require a referral from your primary care physician first, which can add a step but also keeps your care coordinated.

3

Monthly
Premium

The flexibility of a PPO plan usually comes with a higher monthly premium. HMO plans trade some flexibility for a lower monthly cost.

PPO Vs. HMO
Questions, Answered.

What's the difference between a PPO and an HMO?

A PPO (Preferred Provider Organization) plan lets you see in-network and out-of-network doctors and specialists without a referral. An HMO (Health Maintenance Organization) plan generally requires you to use in-network providers and get a referral from your primary care doctor to see a specialist - in exchange, HMO plans typically cost less per month.

Do I need a referral with a PPO plan?

No. One of the main advantages of PPO health insurance is going straight to a specialist without a referral from a primary care doctor.

Is HMO insurance cheaper than a PPO?

Usually, yes. HMO plans tend to have lower monthly premiums than comparable PPO plans, since the network is smaller and care is coordinated through a primary care doctor. Your advisor can compare real quotes for both in your area.

Can I see an out-of-network doctor on an HMO plan?

Generally no, except for true emergencies. HMO plans are built around an in-network provider list, which is part of why they cost less than PPO plans.

Are PPO and HMO plans ACA-compliant?

Yes. Both PPO and HMO are common plan types offered on and off the ACA marketplace. The letters describe how you access care (network and referrals), not whether the plan meets ACA essential health benefit requirements.
