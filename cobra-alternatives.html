Questions, Answered.

How much does COBRA insurance cost compared to alternatives?

COBRA continuation typically runs $2,000-$3,000/month for a family of four because you now pay 100% of the group premium plus up to a 2% admin fee - none of it subsidized by your former employer. Marketplace plans, especially with a subsidy triggered by a lower post-job income, often run $300-$700/month for comparable coverage. Private, underwritten plans fall somewhere in between depending on health history. We model the real numbers for your situation before you decide.

What is the 60-day special enrollment window and why does it matter?

Losing job-based health coverage is a qualifying life event that opens a 60-day special enrollment period for the ACA Marketplace - separate from COBRA's own 60-day election deadline. Missing it means waiting for the next Open Enrollment window, so it's worth comparing your options before that clock runs out, not after you've already signed the COBRA paperwork.

Are COBRA alternatives as good as COBRA coverage?

Often better on network access, sometimes narrower - it depends on the plan. COBRA continues your exact former employer plan, so the network doesn't change. Marketplace and private alternatives can match or exceed that network, and premium private plans from national carriers frequently offer broader specialist access than a regional Marketplace HMO. We compare network overlap for your specific doctors before you switch.

Can I switch from COBRA to a Marketplace or private plan later?

Generally only at your next Marketplace Open Enrollment or if you have another qualifying life event - not any time you choose. That's why comparing cost and coverage during your initial 60-day window, before electing COBRA, is the cheapest time to make this decision.

Will a private or Marketplace plan cover my pre-existing conditions?

Yes. Fully ACA-compliant Marketplace and off-exchange private plans cannot deny coverage or charge more for pre-existing conditions. Short-term medical plans, by contrast, can exclude them - your advisor will flag this clearly before you enroll in anything short-term.

How fast can I get covered if I skip COBRA?

Marketplace plans triggered by a qualifying life event typically start the 1st of the month after you enroll. Private, underwritten bridge plans can be effective as soon as the next day - useful if there's a short gap between your last day of employer coverage and a new plan's start date.

Is the Leo Source advisor service free?

Yes. Our concierge advisors are compensated directly by carriers - there's no separate fee, retainer, or commission added to your premium. You get a side-by-side COBRA comparison and licensed advisor expertise without paying for it.

Who does Leo Source serve?

Leo Source focuses exclusively on Americans ages 18-64, including people who've recently lost job-based coverage and are comparing COBRA against Marketplace and private alternatives.
