Questions, Answered.

What are my health insurance options if I earn too much for ACA subsidies?

If you don't qualify for ACA subsidies, you typically choose between unsubsidized ACA platinum or gold plans, off-exchange private plans with broader national networks, or short-term medical for bridging coverage gaps. A licensed advisor can model true after-tax cost across each route so the decision is grounded in real dollars not sticker prices.

What's the difference between ACA and private plans when you don't qualify for subsidies?

Without subsidies, on-exchange ACA plans often have narrower regional HMO/EPO networks. Off-exchange private plans typically offer wider networks, larger out-of-network benefits, and more carrier choice — at a higher unsubsidized premium. Both cover pre-existing conditions when fully ACA-compliant. We model real total cost (premium + deductible + tax impact) so you can decide.

Can I keep my doctors & specialists with a private plan?

Yes. Premium private plans from carriers like UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Cigna, and Blue Cross Blue Shield include broad national networks with direct specialist access and meaningful out-of-network benefits — important for families who travel, maintain multiple residences, or rely on specific concierge or academic medical center providers.

Can I enroll in a private, off-exchange plan any time of year?

Many private, off-exchange and short-term plans aren't limited to the ACA's Nov–Jan Open Enrollment window and can start same-month or even next-day. Fully ACA-compliant off-exchange plans still generally follow Open Enrollment / qualifying-event rules — your advisor will confirm which route fits your timing.

How can I lower the after-tax cost of premium private coverage?

For self-employed households earning above the subsidy line, the self-employed health insurance deduction is generally above-the-line and can materially reduce after-tax premium cost. Structuring through an S-corp, LLC, or partnership can sharpen the benefit further — our advisors coordinate directly with your CPA so the plan you choose maps cleanly to the deduction you actually capture.

Is COBRA worth it for an unsubsidized family?

Rarely. COBRA continuation typically runs $2,000–$3,000/month for a family of four. A privately underwritten bridge plan often delivers comparable or broader network access at a fraction of the cost — with effective dates as quickly as the next day. We compare both before recommending either.

Is the Leo Source advisor service free if I'm paying full unsubsidized premium?

Yes. Our concierge advisors are compensated directly by carriers — there's no separate fee, retainer, or commission added to your premium, regardless of plan size. You receive licensed advisor expertise without paying for it.

Who does Leo Source serve?

Leo Source focuses exclusively on Americans ages 18–64, with an emphasis on premium private and ACA-compliant coverage for individuals and families who don't qualify for ACA subsidies.
