Bronze, Silver Or Gold?
Marketplace Plan Tiers Explained.

Every plan on the insurance marketplace is grouped into a metal tier - the tier sets your premium-to-out-of-pocket tradeoff, not your quality of care.

B

Bronze — Lowest
Monthly Premium

Bronze marketplace plans cover about 60% of costs on average, with the lowest premium and highest deductible. A fit if you rarely need care and want subsidy dollars to stretch furthest.

S

Silver — Most
Popular Choice

Silver plans cover about 70% of costs and are the only tier eligible for extra cost-sharing reductions if your income qualifies - often the best overall value.

G

Gold — Lowest
Out-Of-Pocket

Gold plans cover about 80% of costs with a higher premium but lower deductible and copays - a fit for frequent care or predictable prescription costs.

Marketplace
Questions, Answered.

What is the Health Insurance Marketplace?

The Health Insurance Marketplace (also called the ACA marketplace or healthcare marketplace) is where individuals and families shop ACA-compliant health plans and, if eligible, apply a premium tax credit to lower the monthly cost. A licensed advisor can walk you through marketplace health plans available in your state.

How do I know if I qualify for a subsidy?

Subsidy eligibility (the premium tax credit) is based mainly on your household income relative to the Federal Poverty Level and whether affordable job-based coverage is available to you. Most people who shop the insurance marketplace qualify for at least a partial credit - a licensed advisor can check your exact number in minutes.

What's the difference between Bronze, Silver and Gold marketplace plans?

The metal tiers set how costs are split between you and the plan, not the quality of care. Bronze covers about 60% of costs with the lowest premium, Silver covers about 70% and is the only tier eligible for extra cost-sharing reductions, and Gold covers about 80% with a higher premium but lower deductible and copays.

When is Open Enrollment for marketplace plans?

Open Enrollment for the 2026 plan year typically runs November 1 through January 15, though exact dates can vary by state. Enrolling by December 15 generally starts coverage on January 1st.

Can I enroll in a marketplace plan outside Open Enrollment?

Yes, if you have a qualifying life event - losing job-based coverage, moving, marriage, or having a baby, among others - you get a 60-day Special Enrollment Period (SEP) to enroll in a health plan marketplace outside the standard window.

Is the Leo Source advisor service free?
