Self-Employed Health Insurance
Questions, Answered.

What is the best health insurance for self employed workers?

There isn't one universal answer - it depends on your income and how much choice you want. Many self-employed and 1099 workers qualify for a subsidized marketplace plan, others prefer a private plan for broader networks, and some use a short-term plan to bridge a gap. A licensed advisor can compare all three against your actual numbers.

Can independent contractors get subsidies on the marketplace?

Yes. Independent contractor health insurance is eligible for the same premium tax credit as any other individual market shopper - eligibility is based on your estimated household income, not how that income is earned.

Can I deduct my health insurance premium if I'm self-employed?

Many self-employed people can deduct 100% of their health insurance premium as an above-the-line tax deduction, which can also affect your marketplace subsidy calculation. Rules depend on your specific situation, so confirm the details with a tax professional.

My freelance income changes every month - how do I estimate it for a subsidy?

You'll estimate your expected annual income for the coverage year; the marketplace reconciles it against your actual income at tax time. A licensed advisor can help you build a realistic estimate so you're not surprised by a repayment or an underused credit later.

What's a short-term medical plan and when should I use one?

Short-term medical (STM) plans offer temporary coverage that can start almost any day, making them a useful bridge between contracts, jobs, or enrollment windows. They're not ACA-compliant and don't cover pre-existing conditions the way marketplace plans do, so they're best as a stopgap, not a long-term plan.

Is the Leo Source advisor service free?
