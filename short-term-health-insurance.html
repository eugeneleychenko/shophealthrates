Short-Term Health Insurance
Questions, Answered.

What is short term health insurance?

Short term health insurance (also called short-term medical or STM) is temporary coverage designed to bridge a gap between long-term plans - for example between jobs, before a new employer's plan activates, or while you wait for the next Open Enrollment Period.

Is short-term medical insurance ACA-compliant?

No. Short-term medical plans are not ACA-compliant. They aren't required to cover the ACA's essential health benefits, can medically underwrite or limit coverage for pre-existing conditions, and may cap benefits. They're built as a temporary bridge, not a replacement for major medical coverage.

How fast can short-term coverage start?

Many short term health insurance plans can start as soon as the day after you apply, since approval is typically based on a short health questionnaire rather than full underwriting.

How long can a short-term plan last?

Terms vary by state and carrier, generally ranging from 30 days up to 364 days, with some plans offering renewal options. Your advisor can confirm what's available where you live.

Is short-term insurance cheaper than COBRA?

Often, yes. Short-term medical premiums are frequently lower than continuing an employer plan through COBRA, though the tradeoff is fewer required benefits and less consumer protection. Your advisor can compare the real numbers for your situation.

Is the Leo Source advisor service free?

Yes. Our concierge advisors are compensated directly by carriers - there's no separate fee, retainer, or commission added to your premium.

Who does Leo Source serve?

Leo Source focuses exclusively on Americans ages 18-64, matching them with the gap, marketplace, or private health coverage that fits their situation.
